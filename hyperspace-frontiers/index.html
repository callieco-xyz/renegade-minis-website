Hyperspace Frontiers is a sci-fi miniatures skirmish game currently in development, focused on tight conflicts between small crews of 5 characters. The setting is explored as an anthology, moving from one frontier to the next, expanding the setting, the characters, and the ways to play as it goes.

The rules are designed to be cinematic, with a sense of momentum to each event. Turns flow in a less rigid way than most skirmish games, and characters are defined by their skills rather than long lists of attributes. The focus stays on what’s happening in the moment and how it unfolds, with the system there to support those beats rather than slow them down.

Core Rules, Crew Guide, and Episodes

The Core Rules, as well as it's companion, the Crew Guide, are available on this website in plain text for free, so you can look through the rules to see what it's like. The Core Rules and Crew Guide are also available as digital downloads in PDF format and to buy as physical prints, both are 30+ pages, fully illustrated booklets.

Beyond these rulebooks, which are more than enough to play the game, there are planned additional releases called Episodes which expand upon the game. Find out more about these in the Episode Guide.

Miniatures, Terrain, and Tokens

The game can be played using any miniatures you like, and the setting is varied enough to make almost anything you can think of work. There are also a range of miniatures already available as printable STL files, with more on the way. Some of these will take the form of multi-part kits, while others will be individually sculpted characters, though even these may have a few different options for weapons and equipment.

As with the miniatures, you can use any terrain you like with the game. The rules for terrain simply apply keywords to parts of terrain to define how they work and this works for most options. Terrain will also be created for the game in future releases, though the focus is currently on miniatures. There are no requirements for any special tokens in the game, so long as you have your battlefield and your crew, some six sided dice and a measuring tape, you're good to go.

Make it your own

One of the most important design goals of this game is that everything about it can be customised. This goes beyond just the crews and the miniatures you collect and paint. The books themselves are customisable. The artwork is designed so that it can be coloured or shaded to create your own unique copy of the game. The Episodes each explore new parts of the frontier, sometimes vastly different, specifically to create new rules that can be combined to create entirely new experiences. You can dive into a rich and expanding setting, or take the ideas and run with it in a different direction.

There's more ideas around this concept I still want to explore, so keep an eye out for what's coming up.
