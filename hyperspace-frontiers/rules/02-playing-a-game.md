## Prepare Your Crew

Players should prepare their crew before playing the game, as they may want time to decide on which Skills and weapons to choose. Unless both players are taking part in the same campaign, both players should have the same crew rating (the combined Level of all your characters). Details on how to create a crew is found in “Hyperspace Frontiers: Crew Guide”.

## Encounters

To play a game you first choose a suitable Encounter, there is an Encounter called "Confrontation" included in the Crew Guide book, as well as in Episodes. Encounters provide structure for your battle, with objectives, victory conditions, and additional rules.

Whichever Encounter you choose, they will all include some basic information:

- Deployment rules explaining where your characters start the game.
- The number and position of Control Points or other terrain elements.
- Rules for how the Encounter is played and how it is scored.

### Setup and Deployment

Following the rules in your chosen Encounter, players should set up their battlefield and characters. After you finish deploying your characters, place a green die next to each with the 6 side showing. This represents their starting Action Points.

## Player Turns

Players roll-off to determine who will go first, the winner of the roll may decide to go first or second. Each player then takes it in turn to activate up to three of their characters.

You don't have to decide at the start of your turn how many characters you will activate. Simply choose a character and activate them. When you have either activated three characters, or have chosen to finish your turn, play moves on to the other player for their next turn.

### Action Points and activations

Every character begins the game with 6 AP, and a green die should be placed next to them showing this value. Throughout the game, you will spend this resource to perform actions.

The first character you activate during a turn must have more than 1 AP remaining. In addition, while you can activate a character that only has 1 AP, doing so will end your turn when their activation is complete, even if you have not yet activated 3 characters. You may not activate the same character more than once in a turn.

### Performing Actions

When you activate a character, first declare which actions you will perform, and the order you will perform them. Each action costs 1 AP to perform and you must have enough for all the actions you declare. Reduce the AP die by the amount spent. If this reduction would reduce the die to 0, it is instead set to 1. If you declare an action and then cannot perform it then the action and the points spent on it are simply lost.

During an activation, your character must spend at least 2 AP, unless they only have one remaining. They may spend a maximum of 4 AP during an activation.

## AP Boosts

Some of the actions your characters perform can result in them regaining Action Points. This is called an AP Boost. If your character receives an AP Boost it increases it's Action Points by 1, up to a maximum of 6.

## Surges

Surges are a pivotal part of the game, reinvigorating your characters to continue the fight a little longer. Players have 2 surges available in each game, and these should be tracked with a suitable token or die. Surges are performed at the start of a turn before you activate any characters.

Once at least one of your characters has reached 1 AP, you are allowed to use one of your Surges. If all of your characters have 1 AP at the start of a turn, you are required to use a Surge if you have one available. Once you have declared a Surge, every character in your crew has their AP restored to 6. You then begin activating characters as normal.

## Rallying

When your characters retreat from the battlefield, they have a chance to return and continue fighting - so long as you have at least 1 Surge remaining.

During a Surge, before restoring your Action Points, each character that has retreated makes a Rallying Roll. A Rallying Roll is a dice test with the size of the dice pool equal to the character's Level. If the number of successes is greater than or equal to the number of characters you still have on the battlefield, the character will redeploy.

Make a Rallying Roll for each character before redeploying those that are successful.

### Redeploying

When your characters rally, they redeploy onto the battlefield. Unless your Encounter states otherwise, characters are redeployed using the same rules for initial deployment. Each character must be placed at least 6" from any enemy characters. If there is no way to place the character without breaking this rule, they cannot be redeployed during this Surge. You may check again the next time you perform a Surge. Characters that are redeployed return without the Grazed or Injured status.

## End Game

If you begin a turn with no characters to activate, because you have no characters remaining on the battlefield or no characters above 1 AP, the game enters its final stages. This is called the End Game. Your turn ends immediately and your opponent gets one final turn. Your opponent may use a surge if they have one. After they have completed their turn, the game ends.

Once the last turn ends it's time to determine the winner. Follow the rules provided by your Encounter to determine the final outcome.
