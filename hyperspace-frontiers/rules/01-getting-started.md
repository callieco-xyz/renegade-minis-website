## What You'll Need

Hyperspace Frontiers is a game for 2 players (usually), so you'll need someone to play against, some miniatures, and a place to play. The rules assume you are using miniatures around 28mm in scale but beyond that you can use any miniatures you like, including some designed specifically for the game. Your miniatures should depict, at least generally, the choices you make while creating your crew.

You will need somewhere to play, a space around 2ft by 3ft, with a clearly marked edge is recommended. You will also need terrain to fill your battlefield as a large open space does not tend to create many interesting moments in a battle. Even a few small pieces is enough to make your battles interesting, though having a good mix of different sized terrain is usually better.

You'll need some six-sided dice for rolling and tracking information. Each player needs 5 green dice, 5 red dice, and 5 black dice for marking action points. The different colours are used to show different states of injury. You'll also need more dice for rolling, around 15-20, ideally in a different colour to avoid confusion. A ruler or tape measure marked in inches is also needed for measuring movement and weapon ranges.

Lastly you'll need a copy of your crew roster, a record of your characters and their abilities, and any books that contain rules you are using: it is a good idea to give your opponent a chance to see what your crew skills and equipment are before playing.

## Dice

There are several rules in the game that require you to roll six-sided dice, usually to determine the outcome of certain actions. No other kinds of dice are used in this game. There are 3 main ways in which these might be used: a straight dice roll, a roll-off, and a test. For all of these rolls, you should make sure your opponent can see the result, before moving or picking up the dice.

### Straight Roll

A straight roll simply involves taking the stated number of dice, rolling them, and then adding up the value. To help avoid confusion a straight roll is usually written as nD6, where n is the number to roll. So 2D6 requires you to roll two six-sided dice, then add the result together. If you are instead asked to roll two D6s, this means each dice rolled is a straight roll. This is usually done when the result of each dice represents a different outcome.

### Roll-off

A roll-off is a quick and easy way to choose between two players, like flipping a coin. Both players roll a single D6 and the player that rolls the highest is the winner. If the roll is a tie the players simply roll again until a winner is found.

### Re-rolls

Sometimes you are permitted to re-roll a die if you do not like the result, for tests or for straight rolls. If you re-roll a die you must accept the new result even if it is worse. A die cannot be re-rolled more than once even if multiple rules allow it to be re-rolled. If you are permitted to re-roll a straight roll that involves more than one dice, you may re-roll any or all dice, but once rolled you may not choose to re-roll any of the other dice.

### Tests

Tests are a little more involved than the other kinds of dice rolls, and are used for actions such as making attacks. In a dice test a player will be instructed to form an initial "pool" of dice, usually taking an amount equal to one of their attributes or something similar. These dice will then be rolled together, the number of dice that show a result of a 4 or higher (4+ are considered successes) #typo. Each test will instruct you on how many successes you need and what happens with the result.

#### Critical Successes

When you roll a test, any dice that show a value of 6 are considered a critical success. Each critical success allows you to roll an additional die for the same test. Each additional die rolled may also result in a critical success and will again allow you to roll additional dice to. There is no limit to how many times this can happen.

## Crews and Characters

The characters you create are the focus of the game. What they can do, and how they interact with each other to control the battlefield and achieve their objectives is key.

Each player commands a crew of 5 powerful characters. While you may use other terms the rules will refer to each individual as a character and the characters belonging to a single player as a crew. Characters belonging to the same crew are called friendly characters, or friends, and characters belonging to your opponent are called enemy characters, or enemies.

Each character has a profile, which contains a few simple attributes, a list of their Skills, and any weapons they are equipped with. These are recorded on a crew roster sheet, which you will find in the "Hyperspace Frontiers: Crew Guide" book, along with all the rules for creating your crew and characters. Many Episodes will also contain additional rules to let you create more unique characters.

### Character Level

The first and most important attribute a character has is it's Level. This is a measure of their overall skill, and gives a quick way to understand how powerful each character is at a glance. A character's Level also determines how many Skills they may have.

### Attack and Defence

A character has both an Attack and Defence attribute, representing their offensive and defensive abilities. It is up to you to decide whether these numbers reflect inherent skill, the use of equipment or armour, or something more unique. These attributes are used when characters make, or are targeted in an attack.

### Skills

Beyond the broad attributes of Attack and Defence, a character is largely defined by their Skills, special abilities that modify what a character can do on the battlefield. For example, they may be exceptionally fast, expert it taking down groups of enemies in close combat, or have special camouflaging abilities that make them impossible to target at a distance.

A list of Skills your characters can take is found in the Crew Guide book, as well as in some of the Episodes. The “Smash” skill is shown below as an example of this:

| Skill     | Rule                                                                         |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Smash** | This character adds the Knockback weapon Trait to one Melee weapon they own. |

## Combat Range

The area surrounding each of your characters, up to 1 inch, is called their Combat Range. Enemy characters cannot move freely into or out of this zone due to the increased threat of attack. In order to enter an enemy's Combat Range, you must perform a Charge Action. To leave Combat Range you must make first make an Attack action.

## Status

At times, your characters will be affected by events or situations on the battlefield which may help or hinder them in their actions. Each of these is represented by a named status. Many rules, Skills, and weapon Traits, use these statuses to create different effects.
#diagrams

| Status    | Condition                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Supported | A character gains the Supported status when they have friendly characters within their Combat Range. This effect is often cumulative, with each friendly character providing a bonus for supporting the character. |
| In Cover  | A character is In Cover when they are targeted in an attack which has an obstructed Line of Sight.                                                                                                                 |
| At Range  | A character is At Range when they are targeted in an attack from more than 6" away.                                                                                                                                |
| Elevated  | A character has the Elevated status in relation to all characters that are on terrain at least 3" lower than their current position.                                                                               |
| Grazed    | A character can gain the Grazed status as a result of taking damage. While a character is Grazed, their Action Points are represented by a red die instead of a green one.                                         |
| Injured   | Injured is a status similar to Grazed, albeit worse, and is gained as a result of taking damage. The Injured status is indicated by the use of a black die for the character's Action Points.                      |

## Weapons

Your characters are armed with a variety of ranged and close-quarters weapons. Weapons, like characters, have a few key pieces of information to tell you how they work: their range, their power, and a list of Traits.

### Range

Weapons come in all shapes and sizes, but can broadly be categorised by the range at which they are used, even if the keyword does not always fit exactly. Weapons can have one of three possible ranges, these are:

| Range | Meaning                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Melee | Melee weapons are only used within Combat Range and have a range of up to 1".                                                                                                                                                        |
| Short | Short range weapons have a range of up to 6". They can also be used within Combat Range but suffer a penalty when doing so.                                                                                                          |
| Long  | Long range weapons cannot be used within Combat Range. They are able to attack targets at any range, but any time they attack a target at a range greater than 6" the character must perform a Focus action first to aim the weapon. |

### Power

Every weapon has a Power attribute that describes how much damage it does. This may be a single powerful blast or a weapon firing a hail of tiny projectiles. This value is added to a character's Attack attribute when making an attack.

### Traits


Weapon Traits, like character Skills, provide additional rules for how a weapon works, giving it unique characteristics and ways to be used. A list of all Weapon Traits is found in the “Hyperspace Frontiers: Crew Guide” book.
