## Move Action

A Move Action allows your character to move up to 6" across the battlefield. Measure the distance your character moves as they travel, as they need to move around, over, or through intervening terrain. 

- You may not move any part of your character's base over the base of an enemy character, and may not finish with your character's base overlapping any other character's base.

- You may not move into the Combat Range of an enemy character during a Move. If you begin your activation within Combat Range of an enemy, you may not move out of it. You may still take a Move action to reposition within the Combat Range of an enemy character.

- Your characters may not move any part of their base out of the battlefield at any time. If any rule would cause them to leave the battlefield, they are removed from the game as though they had retreated.

### Terrain

Battlefields are filled with a variety of terrain that your characters must navigate. To simplify this for your games each piece of terrain is described using 1 or more keywords. Agree with your opponent which terrain items have which keywords before starting your game.

#### Terrain Keyword: Solid

Solid terrain represents objects which cannot be moved over or through. A character must move around Solid terrain counting the total distance travelled as they do so.

#### Terrain Keyword: Climbable

When using terrain with different height levels, your characters need to be able to move between them. If the height change is 1" or less you can ignore the change and measure only the horizontal movement. If the change is greater than 1" you need to use terrain that is Climbable.

When moving to a higher elevation you must subtract 1" of your total allowed movement for each full 3" of height change. When moving down from a higher elevation you may do so without Climbable terrain if the difference in height is less than 3". This still costs 1" of allowed movement, and drops of more than 3" require terrain with the Climbable keyword. You must have sufficient movement remaining to place your character at the new height level.

Depending on your terrain you may decide every part of it is Climbable, usually for stepped hills or rocky terrain. Other pieces may require ladders or steps to ascend. Make sure you agree with your opponent which pieces of terrain are considered Climbable.

#### Terrain Keyword: Obstacle

Obstacles are small objects that present only a small inconvenience to moving: such as rocks, barricades, or crates. A character can move over any Obstacle at a cost of 1" of their allowed movement, in addition to the normal distance travelled.

#### Terrain Keyword: Difficult Ground

Difficult Ground represents areas that require significant effort to cross, such as loose rubble or dense foliage, or anything that causes a slowing effect. If your character wishes to make any part of their movement through Difficult Ground they must pay an additional 1 AP. A character cannot move through Difficult Ground if they only have 1 AP remaining.

## Advance Action

An Advance action is an additional movement action, usually representing a push to make it into cover, or to avoid return fire.

It follows all the same rules as a normal Move action except the character moves a random distance up to D6". If the character also performed a Move action this activation you may re-roll this distance.

A character can use an Advance action to move out of Combat Range of enemy characters. In order to do so they must have performed an Attack action immediately before doing so.

**A character that rolls a 6 for their Advance distance gains an AP Boost.**

## Charge Action

A Charge action is a move that brings you into Combat Range with an enemy. It can’t be performed in the same turn that a Move action is taken. When you begin a Charge action, first declare which enemy character you will target. To determine how far you will be allowed to move, make a straight 2D6 roll, this is your charge distance in inches.

A Charge follows the same rules as a normal Move action, except that you are allowed to enter the Combat Range of the targeted character. You are also allowed to enter the Combat Range of any other enemy character if they are within the Combat Range of your original target. You may not enter the Combat Range of any other enemy characters.

When a character charges, the distance rolled must be sufficient to bring them into Combat Range with their target. If it is not, the charge fails: the character is not permitted to move, and the AP spent on the action is lost.

### Charge Reactions

When you declare your target for a charge, they may react: either they Stand to meet the attack, or they Evade to try and get out of the way.

If the target character declared they would Stand, you may re-roll one of the 2D6 rolled for charge distance. However, a character that chooses to Stand gains +2 to their Defence during this activation.

If the target character declared they would Evade, they may move their character up to 3" following the normal rules for a Move action, before you move your charging character. This means they may not leave the Combat Range of any enemy models they are currently within.

**A character that rolls two 6s for their charge distance gains an AP Boost.**

## Focus Action

The Focus action represents your character taking the time to make an attack with greater precision. You do not have to do anything when your character takes a Focus action, instead a Focus action either allows your character to make an Attack action At Range, or provides a bonus to attacks at closer ranges.

## Attack Action

Attacks are a vital part of the game, and the method for performing one is the same whether you are attacking an enemy from a distance or in close combat. Follow these steps to perform an Attack.

### Step 1: Choose a Weapon and a Target

When you make an Attack you choose one of your character's weapons and choose a target character to attack. The range of your chosen weapon will also affect which enemy character you may target.

- When you have an enemy character within Combat Range, you must choose a Melee or Short range weapon.
- Attacking an enemy outside of your Combat Range, but within 6", you must use either a Short or Long range weapon.
- Attacking an enemy At Range can only be done with a Long range weapon, and only if you have  taken a Focus action

### Step 2: Check Line of Sight

Once you have chosen a weapon and a target, check if you have Line of Sight to them. Line of Sight is determined from the perspective of the character making the attack, meaning you sometimes have to look at things "through their eyes". Check each of the conditions below to determine if you have Line of Sight. 

- If you can't see any part of the target, then you don’t have Line of Sight. You need to be able to see your target.
- If you can see some of the target but any part of it is obscured by another character, then you don’t have Line of Sight. You can't attack through another character.
- If your target is outside of your Combat Range, but have one of your friendly characters in their Combat Range, then you don’t have Line of Sight. You can't shoot into close combat!

If these conditions do not apply, even if the character is mostly obscured, then you have Line of Sight to that target. This includes seeing parts of the model such as weapon barrels, hair, and other minor items.

If your target is obscured by any piece of terrain, but you would otherwise have Line of Sight, then they count as In Cover.

### Step 3: Make Attack Roll

It is time to make an attack roll to see if the weapon hits, and how much damage it does. An attack roll is a test, your dice pool is equal to the Attack attribute of the attacking character plus their chosen weapon's Power attribute. The attack roll will be compared to the target character’s Defence attribute.

The following modifiers affect the Attack roll, these change the size of the attackers dice pool before it is rolled, and the target’s Defence attribute for this attack:

| Modifier | Character | Reason                                                          |
| -------- | --------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| +2       | Attacker  | Character is Elevated compared to their target.                 |
| +2       | Attacker  | Character performed a Focus action and the target is within 6". |
| +1       | Attacker  | For each character giving this character the Supported status.  |
| -2       | Attacker  | Character has spent 4 AP this activation.                       |
| -2       | Attacker  | Character is using a Short range weapon within Combat Range.    |
| +1       | Defender  | For each character giving this character the Supported status.  |
| +2       | Defender  | Character has the In Cover status.                              |

### Step 4: Attack Reactions

When a character is targeted for an attack they may choose one of two reactions: Evade or Stand. A character does not need to choose a reaction.

If the target chooses to Evade, they may move up to 3" following the normal rules for a Move action. After this, if they have In Cover, they gain +1 to their Defence. If, after this move, the attacker would not have Line of Sight for the attack, they still proceed with the attack as though they did, but the target gains +2 to their Defence.

If the target chooses to Stand, and the attack fails to beat their Defence attribute, they gain an AP Boost. However, the target suffers -2 to their Defence for this attack.

### Step 5: Taking Damage

If the attack roll was equal or higher than the target's Defence attribute they take damage. Compare the difference between Attack roll and Defence below:

| Difference | Result                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 0-3        | The target gains the Grazed status. If they are already Grazed they instead become Injured.                                                                                                            |
| 4-5        | The target gains the Injured status. If they are already Grazed, nothing happens. If they are already Injured, they retreat from the battlefield, but may still redeploy using the rules for Rallying. |
| 6-8        | The target retreats. Remove them from the battlefield. They may be redeployed using the rules for Rallying.                                                                                            |
| 9+         | The target retreats, remove them from the battlefield. They may not be redeployed again during the game.                                                                                               |

**If the attacker causes their target to retreat, they gain an AP Boost.**

## Recover Action

Grazed or Injured characters may take a Recover action to attempt to improve their condition so long as there are no enemies in their Combat Range. A character doing so makes a Recover roll, a test with a pool of dice equal to their Defence attribute. If the character rolls 3 or more successes then their status will improve: an Injured character becomes Grazed, and a Grazed character removes the Grazed status.

The character gains +1 for each friendly character that provides them with the Supported status.

**If the character rolls 6 or more successes, they receive an AP Boost.**

## Interact Action

At times your characters may need to interact with elements on the battlefield, or with other characters. The interact action requires a character to have the target of this interaction within their Combat Range to perform, and usually happens automatically.

The specific rules for each kind of interaction is given within the Encounter rules, or from any skills that make use of it.

## Pass Action

Characters are usually required to spend at least 2 AP during their activation. In cases where you have no other actions you wish to perform, you can use the Pass action to meet this requirement.

This action does nothing except use AP and is the only action that can be performed multiple times.
