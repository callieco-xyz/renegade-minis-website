Campaigns in Hyperspace Frontiers allow you to play the same crew from battle to battle. Characters gain experience, increasing their power and skills over time. Campaigns are also a great way to include multiple players and can lead to either fierce competition.

To expand your games into a campaign there are a few extra steps to follow. It is usually a good idea to have a single person nominated as a campaign organiser to manage each of the steps, and to organise the results from each of the games.

The number of players a campaign can include really depends on how much work the organiser, or organisers, are willing to put in. These rules are best for a small number of players. Including a larger number of players is possible, though the campaign organiser may need to take steps to ensure that the match-ups between players are randomised for each round, and that players are getting equal chances to play.

## Campaign Rounds

A campaign is played in a series of rounds, during which every player should play at least 3 games.

During each campaign round, only the first 3 games a player takes part in earn their crew any experience or rewards. The player may continue to take part in additional games and their opponents may gain rewards if it is one of their first 3 games. 

Once all players have completed 3 games the round ends. A campaign can run for as many rounds as you like, though 3-5 rounds is recommended

## Aftermath

After each battle during a campaign, there is an additional stage to the game called the Aftermath. This stage only happens for a player during their first 3 games in a campaign round, and should be done alongside your opponent or campaign organiser.
### Campaign Points

After each game is complete, during the Aftermath stage, players will win a number of campaign points depending on the outcome. These points should be tracked on their crew roster and by the campaign organiser.

## Character Experience

Characters gains experience, or EXP, as they take part in battles. The Crew Roster sheet has an EXP track at the bottom of each character's entry. The EXP track has marked squares along it that indicate a new Level for the character, going up to Level 12. When a character gains EXP simply fill in each space along this track. When you create a character you should fill in the EXP track up to the character's Level.

### Post Battle Experience

After each of your first 3 battles in a campaign round, your crew will earn experience. The total amount of experience is affected by how you want to distribute it among your characters, either spreading it around equally, or focusing it on a limited number of characters. There are 3 options available: Team Effort, Specialisation, or Focused.

To determine how much EXP your crew will gain, take the number of dice shown for your chosen choice, and make a test with them. Each success results in one point of EXP gained. Depending on your choice in the table below you are also allowed to re-roll a number of failed dice rolls. Your crew also gain additional dice to roll if they were underdogs in the battle.

You must distribute the EXP gained as equally as possible between the chosen number of characters, meaning no character can be assigned more than 1 point of EXP more than any other chosen character. A character in your crew also cannot receive any EXP while their current Level is double, or more, than that of your lowest Level character.

| Choice         | No. of characters | No. of dice | Re-rolls | Underdog Bonus |
| -------------- | ----------------- | ----------- | -------- | -------------- |
| Team Effort    | 5                 | 8           | 3        | +4             |
| Specialisation | 3                 | 6           | 2        | +2             |
| Focused        | 1                 | 5           | 1        | 0              |

### Experience Limits

A character in your crew cannot receive any EXP while their current Level is double that of the lowest Level in your crew.

## Levelling Up

Once a character reaches a new Level mark on their EXP track, they will Level Up. Increase the character's Level on the Crew Roster, and then either increase the character's Attack or Defence attribute by 1 point. Remember that the Attack and Defence value can never be set to more than double the other value.

A character that Levels Up also gains 1 point to spend on a new Skill. You can spend this point immediately on a new Skill, retain the point to be be spent during another Level Up, or upgrade an existing Skill to another Skill in the same set. There is a box on each character's entry on your Crew Roster to track any Skill points your character wishes to keep to spend later.

To upgrade a Skill you may remove it from your character, recovering the rank of the Skill as points to be spent on a new Skill. The new Skill must be taken from the same set as the Skill you remove from the character.
## Credits

As well as gaining experience, your crew also earns Credits after each battle, which you can spend at the end of each campaign round on new weapons.

During the Aftermath stage for your first 3 games in a round, after rolling for EXP, make a test with 5 dice. Your crew earns 1 Credit for each success. Record the additional Credits on your crew roster. If your crew were Underdogs during the battle you may re-roll all failed dice during the initial roll.

## Campaign Points

Campaign Points are used to determine the overall winner at the end of your campaign. Your crew earns Campaign Points at the end of the first 3 battles per campaign rounds for the following items:

- +3 campaign points for a draw.
- +6 campaign points for a win.
- +1 campaign points for playing as Underdogs.
- +2 campaign points for winning as Underdogs.

## Crew Ratings and Underdogs

Campaigns will often see crews diverge in terms of their overall power level. This is measured by your Crew Rating, which is simply the levels of all your characters combined.

Whenever you play against a crew that has a Crew Rating 5 points higher than your own, you are considered an Underdog. While this won't help you in the upcoming battle, you will find that your crew gains more experience, credits, and campaign points.

## Campaign Round Conclusion

At the end of each campaign round, before concluding the campaign or moving on to the next round, players have a chance to purchase new weapons for their crew. This should be done during the Aftermath stage after a player’s last game during the round.

### Shopping

Buying new weapons is basically the same as buying weapons for characters when you created them, but the Credits you have available to spend are shared between the entire crew. Each character is still limited to having a maximum of 3 weapons. 

You cannot sell existing weapons but may opt to get rid of them before buying new ones.

### Final Round and Winners

When the final campaign round is complete, each player having had a chance to play at least three battles, the tally of campaign points decides the overall winner, determining which crew claims ultimate victory in the campaign.
