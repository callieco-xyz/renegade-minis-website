Hyperspace Frontiers: Crew Guide

Section One - Creating A Crew

To play Hyperspace Frontiers you assemble a crew of 5 characters. It is up to you whether you want to create an elite squad of well equipped warriors, a ragtag group of plucky adventurers, or maybe something entirely different. The skills and equipment in this book are only a starting point, with far more variety and options available in each of the Episodes.

New Crews

To start creating a crew you’ll need a Crew Roster sheet (found at the end of this book), though it’s usually worth working out what you want on paper first before writing down your choices. You begin with 5 characters each with a Level of 1, and a starting Attack and Defence attribute of 3. You will then work through each character increasing their stats, giving them Skills, and purchasing weapons for them to use.

For a normal crew, you have 16 Levels to spend across all of your characters. Each time you increase the Level of a character you also choose to increase either the Attack or Defence attribute of that character. The only restriction to how this is done are:

  • No character may have a Level that is more than double that of your lowest Level character.
  • No character can ever have their Attack or Defence attributes more than double the other attribute.

If you wish to play a campaign that starts at a lower level, with more room to grow, the starting Levels should be set to 7. If you want to play a higher level crew, especially for one-off games with bigger and bolder skills, the starting Levels should be increased to 26.

Character Skills

Once you have set the Level of each of your characters you can then choose their Skills. There are a range of Skills on the next few pages to choose from. Each Skill provides the character with abilities and bonuses that will determine how they fare on the battlefield.

Character can be given up to 4 Skills each, and may not take the same Skill twice. Skills also have an associated rank based on how powerful it is, and characters may select Skills up to a combined rank equal to their Level.

Skill Sets and Alignment

Skills are organised into sets representing specific roles on the battlefield. You don’t have to choose from just one set for your Skills, but doing so provides a small bonus.

When all of a character’s Skills are from the same set they gain access to that set’s Alignment Skill. This is a free Skill that can’t be taken any other way, and the character keeps it so long as they do not have Skills from any other set. An Alignment Skill is shown with a rank of A instead of a number.

Skill Set: Scouts

Scouts are highly independent warriors that range ahead of the rest of your crew, securing key objectives, and disrupting enemy plans. They favour speed and evasiveness over brute force, preying on weak points before slipping out of reach of any response.

Skill Rank Description
Runner 1 After making an Advance action, this character may immediately perform a second free Advance action.
Vanguard 1 When being deployed, or redeployed, this character can be placed up to 9” from their deployment position. They may not be placed within Combat Range of an enemy character.
Acrobatic 2 This character ignores movement penalties for terrain that would reduce their movement distance or require them to spend additional AP to move. This character always gains +2 to their Defence for choosing an Evade reaction to an Attack, regardless of any changes to Line of Sight.
Mark Target 2 If this character targets an enemy in an Attack At Range that does cause them to take damage, every subsequent attack against that same character gains +2 to the Attack roll for the rest of your turn.
Stealth Field 2 This character cannot be targeted for an Attack At Range. This character also adds 3” to their Advance distance.
Fly 3 This character ignores all intervening terrain and characters when moving and may move up to 12” during a Move action. When making an Attack At Range this character always counts as Elevated. When being Attacked At Range this character has +2 to their Defence.
Scout A This character gains +1” to all Move, Advance and Charge distances.

Skill Set: Champions

Champions lead from the front to inspire their crew mates or intimidate the enemy forces. They meet the conflict head on, pressing the attack and setting the pace of battle before the enemy can gain a solid footing. Striding through enemy fire they engage the most powerful enemy forces in hand to hand combat, preferably where everyone can see them.

Skill Rank Description
Grit 1 The character adds +3 to their recovery rolls.
Smash 1 This character adds the Knockback weapon Trait to one Melee weapon they own.
Akimbo 2 This character may perform 2 Attack actions if they are equipped with 2 short range weapons. These may be the same weapon but two must be purchased. Each attack must use a different short range weapon. Each attack must be declared at the start of the character’s activation, and costs 1 AP as normal.
Brawler 2 If this character is in Combat Range of more than one enemy character, but without the Supported status, it gains +2 to it’s Attack roll.
Duellist 2 When this character attacks a character with a higher Level using a Melee weapon, they add +2 to their Attack roll.
Bastion 3 This character has +2 to their Defence attribute, when Grazed or Injured it becomes +3. If they are forced to retreat from the battlefield and subsequently redeploy, this is reduced to +1 regardless of their status.
Frenzy 3 If this character is only equipped with Melee weapons it treats results of 5+ as a critical success instead of 6. This character also rolls 3D6 for a charge distance and uses the two highest results for their distance.
Champion A This character gains +1 to their Attack attribute, until the end of your turn, after performing a Charge action.

Skill Set: Enforcers

Enforcers rely on discipline, planning, and methods that have been tested under pressure, keeping operations grounded when others drift toward theatrics. Enforcers apply force with deliberate efficiency and a quiet suspicion of anything that calls itself heroic.

Skill Rank Description
Armoured 1 This character gains +2 to it’s Defence when targeted in an Attack.
Commander 1 Any time this character, or a friendly character within 6” of them, gain an AP Boost, that AP Boost may instead be given to any other character in your crew.
Push Back 1 This character may add the Knockback trait to any long range weapon they are equipped with.
Shields 2 Attack rolls against this character do not count rolls of 6 as a critical success.
Dominate 2 Enemy characters within 6” of this character cannot gain AP from an AP Boost. If an enemy character would gain an AP Boost within 6”, this character instead gains one.
Pin Down 2 When this character attacks with a Short or Long ranged weapon, from outside of their Combat Range, any target that takes damage also loses 1 AP. This cannot reduce the target’s AP below 1.
Sharpshooter 3 If this character performs a Focus action before an Attack At Range, they may re-roll all initial dice in the Attack roll. This does not apply if the target is within Combat Range.
Tracker 3 This character always counts as having performed a Focus action when making an Attack At Range.
Enforcer A This character may re-roll one failed dice in every Attack roll.

Skill Set: Wardens

Wardens hold the line, anchoring their crew with the unwavering belief that simply standing their ground will win the day. If they are eventually, grudgingly, forced to give way, they turn retreat into something ordered and deliberate, yielding space without ever surrendering control.

Skill Rank Description
Opportunity 1 This character receives an AP Boost for causing a character to become Injured or Retreat when making an Attack within Combat Range.
Relentless 1 When this character activates with only 1 AP remaining they may spend up to 2 AP for their activation instead.
Aegis 2 This character, and any character gaining Supported from it, gains +2 to their Defence attribute when targeted in an Attack At Range.
Comeback 2 The first time this character retreats they will automatically redeploy when you next Surge, they also gain +2 to their Attack or Defence when they redeploy. The player decides which attribute to improve when they redeploy.
Fallback 3 After you have used your final Surge, this character, and any friendly characters within 6” of them, gain +2 to their Attack and Defence attributes.
Restoration 3 This character can perform an interact action targeting a friendly character or themselves. Make a test using this character’s Level. If they roll 3 or more successes the targeted character removes any Grazed or Injured status. They may perform this action twice in an activation.
Warden A Enemy characters that begin their activation within 6” of this character reduce all Move, Advance, and Charge actions for their activation, by 2”.

Weapons and Equipment

Each of your characters must be equipped with weapons to use in their battles. All characters have access to the “Unarmed” weapon and you can purchase additional weapons using Credits. Not including the Unarmed weapon, characters may have up to 3 weapons.

Each character in a new crew has 25 Credits to spend on weapons. These Credits are not transferable to other characters. You also have an additional 30 Credits to spend on weapons which you can divide among your characters as you like.

Any Credits from this last portion that are left over can be kept and recorded on your crew roster. For one-off battles this is of no benefit but can be used in Campaigns.

Weapon Traits

Traits, like the Skills your characters have, can significantly change how a weapon operates. They make your weapons hit harder, shoot faster, or knock your opponents clean off their feet. The rules for each Trait is listed below, with additional Traits found in Episode releases.

Trait Rule
Accurate You may re-roll any failed dice in the initial roll of an Attack roll.
Armour Piercing If this weapon would cause a character to become Grazed they instead become Injured. This does not count as the Grazed result on the damage table.
Impact When this weapon targets a character, it also targets all other characters within their Combat Range. Make your attack roll once as normal, then check if it would cause damage to all targeted characters.
Destructive Critical successes with this weapon allow you to roll 2 additional dice instead of 1.
Executioner If this weapon would cause a character to become Injured they instead retreat. This does not count as the Grazed result on the damage table.
Heavy This weapon cannot be used if the character has declared a Move or Advance action this activation.
Knockback An attack with this weapon causes the target to be moved backwards after damage has been resolved. They move a number of inches equal to the difference in the Attack roll. The direction they travel is determined by drawing a line from the centre of the attacking model towards the centre of the target model, the model moves along this line away from the attacker. If the character encounters any terrain, the edge of the battlefield, or other characters during this movement, the character stops moving.
Light This weapon does not require the character to perform a Focus action to be used At Range.
Rapid Fire A character that makes an attack with this weapon against a target within 6”, and has taken a Focus action, adds +2 to the Power of the weapon.
Reach A character with this weapon may move up to 2” before making an Attack action. They may move into Combat Range of an enemy character but otherwise treat this as a normal Move action with the normal restrictions.
Stun A character that would take damage from this weapon also loses 1 AP for a Grazed result, and 2 AP for an Injured result. However, an attack from this weapon can never cause a character to retreat.
