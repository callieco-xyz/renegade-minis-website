Hyperspace Frontiers: Crew Guide

Section Two - Encounters

Before playing a game you must choose an Encounter to play. An Encounter provides additional rules for your game that dictate how to set up your battlefield and crew, and determines the overall objectives for the players. Below are details on the most common elements each Encounter will include.

Battlefield Setup

Most Encounters let you set up your battlefield with any terrain you like. It is generally a good idea to include a variety of terrain that creates choice for each player. This usually means areas that block Line of Sight and provide cover, as well as elevated positions to counter them. It is up to you whether one player or both will decide on the position of any terrain not dictated by the Encounter.

Some Encounters are based around key pieces of terrain and will instruct you to place them in a specific position. If you do not have exactly the right kind of terrain you should do your best to approximate it, and feel free to modify the Encounter to suit what you have available.

Deployment

After your battlefield is set up you need to place your characters onto the battlefield, this is called deployment. Unless otherwise stated by the Encounter rules, you should deploy your characters as follows:

Choose Position

Encounters include deployment positions for each player, usually on opposite sides of the battlefield. If one player set up the terrain then the other player gets to choose which position they want. Otherwise players roll-off with the winner choosing which position they prefer.

Zones, Table Edges, and Entry Points

A deployment position is usually defined as one of three types:

  • An area, or ‘zone’, usually described by it’s distance from the table edges. If your deployment position is given as a zone you must place your characters so they are completely within the given area.
  • One of the table edges of your battlefield, usually identified as either the long or short edges. If you are using a square battlefield you may decide which ones these refer to. If your deployment position is a table edge you must place your characters so that they are on the battlefield with their base touching the given table edge
  • An entry point. These are markers placed on the battlefield during setup and should be no larger than 30mm in diameter. When you deploy your characters they must be within 6” of the entry point.

Deploy Characters

Once the deployment positions are chosen the players should roll-off again to see who goes first. The first player deploys all of their characters onto the battlefield, followed by the second player. You are now ready to begin the game.

Control Points

Control Points represent areas on the battlefield of significance. While a Control Point can represent many things, on the tabletop they are typically represented by markers or small terrain pieces around 30-40mm. You may need up to 6 markers and should be able to identify them with a number between 1 and 6.

Contesting Control Points

Each Control Point has an area around it extending 3” from it’s centre, called it’s Influence. While a character is within this range it is considered to be Contesting the Control Point. A Control Point can be Contested by more than one character at a time.

Securing Control Points

Control Points can be Secured by characters so long as the Control Point’s Influence does not contain any enemy characters. The character needs to perform an Interact action on the Control Point, requiring them to have it within their Combat Range. So long as there are no enemies in the Control Point’s Influence the Interact action succeeds and the Control Point is Secured.

A Control Point remains Secured so long as no enemy characters enter it’s Influence. As soon as any enemy character is Contesting it the Control Point stops being Secured. A Control Point cannot be Secured whilst it is already Secured.

Objectives

In order to determine a winner for each game an Encounter will include objectives. These can range from something as simple as “The player that causes the most characters to retreat” to more complex outcomes based on a range of goals. These will be listed in your Encounter.

Victory Points

The most common way for an Encounter to determine the winner is by awarding Victory Points for completing a range of objectives throughout the game. The player with the most Victory Points at the end of the game being the winner. You will need to keep a track of Victory Points as you earn them to avoid any errors.

Encounter: Confrontation

This Encounter sees two crews go head-to-head on the battlefield, their only goal: to establish complete dominance.

Setup and Deployment

Set up terrain on your battlefield as normal. Players then alternate placing 6 Control Points on the battlefield. These must be 3” from any table edge, and 6” from other Control Points, otherwise they can be placed anywhere.

Players roll-off, the winner chooses choose any of the 4 table edges as their deployment position. The other player takes the table edge opposite the first player as their deployment position. Once players have their deployment position they must check which Control Point is closest to their table edge. This Control Point is considered owned by that player. If there is more than one Control Point at the same distance, the player may choose which one they wish to own.

Players now roll-off to see which player will take the first turn.

Victory Points

Throughout the game, players can scores Victory Points for the following:

Objective Victory Points
Cause an enemy character to retreat from the Battlefield. 2
Contest the Control Point owned by your opponent during a turn where you perform a Surge. 6
Have at least 2 Control Points Secured during a turn where you perform a Surge. 4
Contest the Control Point owned by your own crew during a turn where you perform a Surge. 1

Dynamic Objectives

The goals of a crew can shift as the battle progresses. To represent this you may include Dynamic Objectives in your games in addition to the objectives of your chosen Encounter. Both players must agree to using Dynamic Objectives. If your Scenario does not use Victory Points or Control Points, they cannot be used.

At the start of the game, both players should roll twice on the table below to select two random objectives, re-rolling duplicates. Each objective has a condition, when the player achieves the condition, they score the Victory Points shown in the table. These objectives are not kept secret.

When you score one of these objectives, roll on the table again to replace it with a new one. When you perform a Surge you may choose to discard an incomplete objective and roll on the table again to replace it.

D6 Result Objective Condition Victory Points
1 Take them down! Name one of the character’s on the opposing team, cause them to retreat from the battlefield. 3
2 Hold the line! Contest at least half of all the Control Points at the start of one of your turns. 2
3 Push them back! Cause an enemy character, who is contesting a Control Point, to retreat from the battlefield. 2
4 Never back down! Have 3 or more Grazed or Injured characters at the start of one of your turns. 3
5 Show them what we’re made of! Do not choose an Evade reaction to Charges or Attacks until your next Surge. This objective is lost when you next perform a Surge whether you achieve it or not. 2
6 Take the fight to them! Make a successful Charge action with 3 or more of your characters before your next Surge. This objective is lost when you next perform a Surge whether you achieve it or not. 3
